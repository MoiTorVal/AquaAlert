# Phase 7.6 — RCD / UCCE Cohort Partnership Plan

Goal: one RCD (Resource Conservation District) partner hosting a 15–30 farmer
pilot for one season, documented in an MOU usable as a grant exhibit, and a
joint WETA application with the RCD as fiscal sponsor.

## Target shortlist (verify current contacts before sending — districts
## reorganize and staff turn over; check each website + SGMA portal listing)

Priority = Central Valley districts inside critically-overdrafted SGMA basins,
where extraction reporting pressure makes AquaAlert's SGMA export immediately
useful.

| # | District | Why |
|---|----------|-----|
| 1 | East Kaweah GSA area / Tulare County RCDs | Kaweah subbasin is critically overdrafted; SGMA reporting burden high; matches our Kaweah-template export target |
| 2 | Madera County RCD | Madera subbasin critically overdrafted; many small/under-resourced farms |
| 3 | East Stanislaus RCD | Active irrigation-efficiency programming; mid-Valley reach |
| 4 | West Stanislaus RCD | Same basin pressure, complementary coverage |
| 5 | Cachuma RCD (Santa Barbara) / Central Coast alternate | Central Coast option if Valley districts stall; strong small-farm equity angle |

Also: UC Cooperative Extension (UCCE) irrigation advisors for Tulare, Fresno,
and Madera counties — advisors can validate the agronomy and co-sign outreach.

## Sequence

1. Pick #1 target; tailor the cold email (below) with their named programs.
2. Send 5 RCD emails + 2–3 UCCE advisor emails in week one; follow up at day 7.
3. First call: 20 min demo (farm detail traffic light + /impact dashboard +
   SGMA PDF export — that's the whole pitch).
4. Offer: free for the pilot cohort; RCD gets aggregate impact numbers for
   their own reporting; farmers get alerts + SGMA reports.
5. Sign MOU (template in `mou-template.md`), even informal — grant exhibit.
6. Draft WETA application jointly, RCD as fiscal sponsor.

## Cold email template (EN)

Subject: Free water-stress alerts + SGMA reports for [District] farmers — pilot offer

Hi [Name],

I'm building AquaAlert, a free tool that texts small farmers a green/yellow/red
alert before their crops hit water stress, using OpenET satellite data and the
FAO's AquaCrop model — no sensors, no agronomist required. It also generates
their SGMA extraction reports (CSV/PDF) automatically from logged irrigations,
and it works in English and Spanish.

I'm looking for one district partner to host a 15–30 farmer pilot this season,
free for everyone involved. [District] came up first because [specific reason:
subbasin status / named program]. The district would get cohort-level water
savings numbers for its own reporting; I'd get field validation.

Could I show you a 15-minute demo this month?

[Name] · [phone] · aqua-alert demo: [public /impact URL]

## Follow-up cadence

- Day 7: short bump ("happy to send a 2-min video instead").
- Day 14: final note + move to next district on the list.

## What to log for the grant narrative

- Dates of outreach, response rates, demo dates — WETA reviewers like traction.
- Any letter of support, even pre-MOU.
